\begin{abstract}
\noindent \textbf{Summary:} This report presents a comprehensive approach to forecasting anonymized hedge-fund panel time series, focusing on a dataset of 5.3 million observations across 36,923 unique series with 86 features. \\
\textbf{Problem Statement:} The objective is to accurately predict future returns while strictly avoiding look-ahead bias, evaluated under a highly concentrated weighted skill score. \\
\textbf{Methods Used:} A rigorous pipeline combining classical statistical models (AR, MA, ARMA, ARIMA, SARIMA) and deep sequence learning (RNN, GRU, LSTM) is implemented. All modeling respects critical EDA findings, particularly 100\% stationarity after first differencing. \\
\textbf{Key Findings:} Results demonstrate that simple baselines and low-order classical models perform surprisingly well on stable series, while deep sequence models (LSTM/GRU) and gradient-boosted trees offer superior performance on volatile and high-weight series. Extreme weight concentration (top 1\% of rows contribute 65\% of the score) dictates that models must be optimized using weight-aware objective functions.
\end{abstract}
